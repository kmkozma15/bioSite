My Childhood

I was born and raised in Playas de Tijuana, Mexico. Playas de Tijuana is located near Mexicali and the border of San Diego. While growing up, I lived with my mother, father, and fraternal twin sister, Pamela. We grew up in a home overlooking the beach. Though we did not visit the beach too often, my favorite memory as a child, is getting together with my cousins every Saturday and playing games with them.

Whether I am repairing, researching, or tinkering with electronics, I love building new things. There is always something new I learn from old projects I have worked on. My favorite way to pass time is by creating new things that solve old problems.

During my college years, photography has opened new doors for me. I have met amazing people, experienced new places and honed my skill as a photographer. I have won multiple photography contests and I enjoy going to cinema expos and testing out the latest equipment.

Throughout my childhood, my father has taught me everything I know today about auto mechanics. He has repaired many cars and I have helped him throughout the process. He has taught me how to weld radiators, fix a flat tire, and repair engines.

Where I am Going

"Today, I am the Equipment Coordinator for Amazon Fulfillment Center. I oversee all incoming and outgoing technology within the warehouse. I repair computers and troubleshoot equipment issues associates may be experiencing. I am thankful for my position and the opportunity to learn alongside a global company. Going forward, my goal is to continue growing alongside the Amazon Information Technology department and take part in their Associate-to-Tech program. I plan on becoming an IT Technician so I may further support my associates and continue doing what I love. Without the love and support from my partner, friends and family, I do not know where I would be."
